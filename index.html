FOOD JOURNEY IN LONDON

THE SERIES OF ILLUSTRATION HAS BEEN SHORTLISTED IN V&A ILLUSTRATION AWARD 2019.

Inspired by the experience working in Domaine de Boisbuchet in France. I was aware and curious about the relation between food and human in the city. In the project, I interviewed people and joined projects related to food issue in London. They are Refettorio Felix at St. Cuthberts, Hackney City Farm, Stepney City Farm- Furry Tale, Foodcycle Peckham, Glengall wharf community garden, Toast Ale, Rubies in the Rubbles, Oddbox, Organiclea. From the origin to the waste, I started the journey to seek for various kind of relationship between human and food, and rethink the value of food.

作品記錄倫敦境內的永續飲食，研究議題包含剩食廚房，剩食產品以及都市農場與菜園。我訪問十餘處關注剩食議題的不同組織，並參與廚房志工。將田野結果繪製成一 系列報導插畫。希望能提供一個溫暖與趣味的方式，瞭解食物浪費議題。

THE BUS CONNECTED CITY

THE WORK HAS BEEN LISTED MERIT IN 3*3 ILLUSTRATION AWARD 2019.

London is a multicultural city, traveling on the bus moving from one place to another is like traveling in different world. Empty fried chicken box on the top of bus stand in south east London, chestnut vendors along the river Thames, Indian dessert selling in the park near Whitechapel, Kosher food and jewish culture in North London... Food from different area represents different cultural. For me, buses play the role of connecting all the bit and pieces in the city together.

過去倫敦的生活經驗，讓我覺得城市最可貴是對多元種族文化的包容。在公車上，乘客都說著不同語言; 乘著公車穿越非裔區到猶太區、印度區、金融區，每換一區彷彿抵達不同國家。創作以飲食作為文化的線索，記錄城市裡多元文化共融的景象。

British Seasonal Fruit and Vegetable

"Nowadays in UK no one cares about seasonal food anymore. Fruit and vegetables are importing from European countries, there's no obvious seasonal difference anymore."
A friend told me this while I was mentioning seasonal production in the U.K.. In Taiwan, I can easily tell the season change by noticing the different fruits and vegetables in the market, whereas in the U.K. I can barely notice the difference.I started to do this series of collage with the hope that people would be aware of them again.

農業種植技術與保存技術進步，漸漸感受到人類與自然的連結漸漸疏離，從順應自然到操控自然。希望藉由製作這系列的年曆，時常提醒自己與年曆使用者當季正盛產的蔬果，上市場時能更有意識地購買。

咦！是什麼呢？
WOW! WHAT IS THIS

The picture book Hey! What is It?, based on the puppet play “Story of Solar Terms Fruits Projects: Summer” by Yinalang. Group in 2021, focuses on Taiwanese fruits in season, depicting the seasonal imagery from various perspectives such as fruits, land, and memories.
Invited by the troupe — Yinalang to join their field research, we walked into the farmland observing the growth and ripeness of watermelon, and joined the harvest work in the summer of 2021. The experience leads us to reflect on the relationship between humans and nature. The book aims to inspire children to be more conscious of a sustainable food system.

HI I AM YING

I am a Taiwanese illustrator, currently based in Taiwan.
I grew up in the mushroom farm which owned by my grandfather, I guess that's part of the reason I am fascinated by food and plants. Growing herbs and baking pastries are my new meditations.
Therefore, food and plants are mainly my illustration topic.
I also enjoy meeting people from different background.
This year, I started to focus on reportage illustration. Combining the interviews and illustration, my aim is to bring more public awareness to different issues we currently encountering in our society.

王盈穎，插畫工作者，現居台灣。
畢業於倫敦藝術大學視覺插畫研究所，作品多為拼貼媒材，主題專注於食物、環境與自然節氣。作品曾入圍2019英國維多莉亞與亞伯特博物館插畫獎．（V&A Illustration Award ） 、美國3x3 插畫獎。
2018年於倫敦始發展餐車紀實插畫，聚焦城市剩食、經濟動物與季節性作物議題．透過走訪城市中的菜園與農場、搜集故事與案例，發展一系列紀實插畫．2019年回到台灣，開始專注環境與食物關係．從風土節氣的日常作物、都市裡可食用的野草採集．到實踐野草廚房烹調，希望透過五感體驗結合插畫，提高人們對於周遭環境的感知與對問題的意識。
